How does a Spanish Wedding look like?

This wedding is a spanish day wedding. This means that the wedding occurs during midday, more or less. After the wedding, all guest are invited to a cooktail while the bride and groom are signing some papers and taking photos. The cooctail normally serve a surpluse of very nice munchies/canapés together with wine/beer or whatever and our weddings will be no exception. The bride and groom join in at the cooktail as soon as they are done with signing papers and taking fotos. The cooctail last for around 2-3 hours and then its time for lunch. The lunch starts around 17 and might end at 19-20 where the dinner gradually transition into a dance floor/party. The openbar will be open unti late and if you are still in the mood the busses will take you back to Logrono/Torrecilla

What should I wear?

Spaniards like to dress up and almost always goes very fancy to events. For dayweddings, the dresscode is not as stright as a night wedding but should be fancy. Guys do not notice any differenc between the two but at a night wedding girls wear long dressses and a tad bit less color. I was looking around for "dress code" for.

Checklist for Swedes

  • You should probably dress a bit more fancy than what would be proper in Sweden
  • Sleep in, it is going to be a long day so make sure you get as much sleep as you can, it is a non stop event for 12h
  • You should arrive a bit hungry. There is going to be a lot of good food, you do not want to miss out and we already payed for it
  • Be aware of the open bar. There is no need to run, as I mentioned, you have 12 hours
